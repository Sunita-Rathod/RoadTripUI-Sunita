Kedarnath is a town located in the Rudraprayag district of the northern state of Uttarakhand, India. It is one of the holiest pilgrimage sites for Hindus, particularly for followers of Lord Shiva. Kedarnath is situated in the Garhwal Himalayas, at an elevation of about 3,583 meters (11,755 feet) above sea level.


Kedarnath Temple: The town is most famous for the Kedarnath Temple, an ancient and sacred Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is part of the Char Dham Yatra, a pilgrimage circuit that also includes Badrinath, Yamunotri, and Gangotri.


Panch Kedar: Kedarnath is one of the Panch Kedar, a group of five Shiva temples in the Garhwal region. The other four are Madhyamaheshwar, Tungnath, Rudranath, and Kalpeshwar.
